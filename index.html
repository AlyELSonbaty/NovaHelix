Why we exist

Good research questions are common. Finding the right people to work on them is not. Nova Helix closes that gap.

A

Leads come from anywhere

A research lead can be a question, a dataset, or an early hypothesis — submitted by anyone with something worth investigating.

B

Researchers choose their orbit

Researchers browse open leads in their field and join the ones they're equipped to move forward, regardless of institution or geography.

C

Fully remote, by design

No office, no home country — just the collaboration. It's how we work across 25 countries without asking anyone to relocate.

How it works

One lead. An orbit of collaborators.

Every project starts the same way: a lead at the center, and researchers who attach themselves to it one by one.

Enters orbit

A lead is proposed

Submitted with the question, what's known so far, and what kind of expertise it needs.

Attaches

Researchers join

Matched by field and availability, researchers opt in — usually a small team, not a crowd.

Stabilizes

The paper takes shape

The team works remotely and asynchronously toward a submission-ready manuscript.

In progress

Three papers, in motion right now

We're a young collective — nothing is published yet, and we'd rather be honest about that than pretend otherwise.
